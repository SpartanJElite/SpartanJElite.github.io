GLEE Science Intern

NASA's Colorado Space Grant Consortium

(March. 2020 - Present)

Taking inspiration from NASA's Apollo Moon landings 50 years ago, the Great Lunar Expedition for Everyone (GLEE) will be a catalyst for a new generation of space missions and explorers. This scientific and technological mission to the Moon will deploy 500 LunaSats to the lunar surface to conduct local and distributed research by partnering with NASAâ€™s 2023 Artemis Mission to the Moon. LunaSats are tiny spacecraft with an integrated sensor suite that will be programmed by teams of students, from every member country of the United Nations, to a mission of their own design. These teams will be mentored by GLEE program staff through the entire design, build, test, launch, and data gathering process.

Working within an team setting, Science Team members within GLEE lead the devlopment of embedded hardware, science analysis, and worked with NASA on goals for the project. This includes testing and verifying side variety of sensors including Magnetometer, Accelerometer , Capacitive, Thermopile, and Radio Frequency Seensors that will be included in the final LunaSat Architecture. I lead the develoment of the capactive sensor in order to find the density and dieletric constant of the lunar surface.

    Projects

    Project STAY

    The Stablize Thruster Attitude control all Yearlong project is a attitude control system for a rocket. This is a personal project of mine that I've been excited about. The STAY projects is a gimbal system with two servos controling the nozzle of the rocket. This controls it for the pitch and yaw portion of rocket's attitude. The propusion aspect of the rocket is Estes C6-3 Engine. The goal for STAY is to ensure that the rocket and launch and land using only the nozzle gimbal system as its only means of control and stablization. This was inspired by SpaceX's StarShip. The github has all the documentation of the project with videos and pictures.

    Project LEO

    The Longitude/Latitude Euler Orientation project is a attitude control system for a rc plane. This is a personal project of mine that I've been excited about. The LEO project is a self stablizing plane that can take any perubation and be stablize through it's control surfaces. The control software should about to handle more perubations and its goals for the plane to remain in level steady flight. The control system controls 5 control mechanism (4 control surface, 1 thrust controller) which is the 2 aileron control surfaces, 1 rudder control surface, and 1 elevator control surface. The RC plane that the flight software is a conditional configuration propeller plane. The github has all the documentation of the project with videos and pictures.
